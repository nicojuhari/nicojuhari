## Recommended Outline

**H1: 5 Signs Your Excel Has Become a Business Risk (Not Just an Inconvenience)**

**Intro:** Brief story hook — not abstract. The finance company where one person held all the data. What happened when they were unavailable. Don't name the company — set the scene. Lead into: "Most business owners treat Excel as an inconvenience. Here's when it becomes a risk."

**H2: Sign 1 — Only One Person Can Access or Update the Data**
- The single point of failure risk
- Real numbers: what happens when that person is sick, quits, or makes an error
- Not a collaboration complaint — a business continuity problem

**H2: Sign 2 — You Can't Tell Where a Number Came From**
- Audit trail problem
- Formula error examples (JPMorgan $6.2B — but contextualize for SMB scale)
- 88-94% of spreadsheets contain errors (cite the stat)
- For finance/loan tracking/inventory: this is a liability

**H2: Sign 3 — Reports Take Hours Instead of Minutes**
- Manual compilation = person-dependent = bottleneck
- What "instant reports" actually means for decision-making
- Use the case study: "reports went from zero to instant"

**H2: Sign 4 — Data Entry Happens More Than Once**
- Double-entry = error rate × 2
- Client entered in Excel → re-entered somewhere else → mismatch happens
- Time cost: how many hours/week? Ask the reader to calculate.

**H2: Sign 5 — Your Excel Lives in One Location (or One Person's Computer)**
- Data portability problem
- "Available on any device, anywhere, 24/7" as contrast
- GDPR/data security angle (brief, not deep)

**H2: What to Do About It**
Brief options framework:
1. No-code tool (Knack, Airtable) — when it works, when it doesn't
2. Custom web app — when the process is too specific for off-the-shelf
3. The finance-fluent developer difference (soft CTA — not salesy)

**CTA:** "If one or more of these signs describes your business, let's talk. I've replaced Excel systems for finance and operations teams — I know what it costs and what you get."

## Angle
Lead with risk, not efficiency. The audience has been told "Excel isn't collaborative" a thousand times. That's not what moves them. Risk does: "If this person leaves tomorrow, what happens to your data?" Finance-fluent angle: name the specific risks that a finance or operations professional would recognize — audit trail problems, formula errors, single-point-of-failure.
