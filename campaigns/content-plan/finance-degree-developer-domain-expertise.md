## Recommended Outline

**H1: I Have a Finance Degree and Build Business Software. Here's Why That Changes Everything.**

**Intro:** The moment that crystallized it — sitting across from a business owner explaining their Excel system. Every other developer would have heard "loan tracking, income, expenses, multi-user access." I heard the underlying question: "How do I make a decision in 10 minutes that currently takes 2 hours?" The software is different when you understand what the business actually needs vs. what was spec'd.

**H2: The Translation Gap in Custom Software**
- The most common reason custom software fails: it was built correctly but solves the wrong problem
- Developer hears: "I want a form that saves to a database"
- Business owner needs: "I want to see which clients are 30 days behind on payments in one click"
- Finance/business training closes that gap

**H2: What Changes When the Developer Understands Finance**
- Specific examples:
  - A loan tracking system where the developer knows what "arrears" means — and builds the alert logic accordingly
  - An expense app where the developer knows the difference between cash and accrual accounting — and asks the right question before designing the report
  - A multi-user system where the developer knows why the finance director and the loan officer need different views
- This isn't a credential — it's practical knowledge that changes what questions get asked in the brief

**H2: Why Most Developers Don't Ask This Question**
- Dev training is tech-first: "what do you want built?"
- Business training is outcome-first: "what decision are you trying to make faster?"
- The gap is structural — most developers haven't worked in a business before they started building for businesses

**H2: When It Matters Most (and When It Doesn't)**
- Matters most: finance operations, business process tools, reporting dashboards, anything where the underlying logic is domain-specific
- Less critical: marketing sites, ecommerce storefronts, standard CRUD apps
- Be honest: for a simple website, any good developer is fine. For a system that will manage your business data? Domain knowledge changes the outcome.

**H2: What This Means for You**
- One meeting instead of three revision cycles
- Software that maps to how the business actually makes decisions
- Less time explaining your process — more time seeing it built correctly

**CTA:** "If you're building something that needs to understand your business first — let's talk."
