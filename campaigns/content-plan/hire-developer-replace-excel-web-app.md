## Recommended Outline

**H1: How to Hire a Developer to Replace Your Excel: What to Expect, What It Costs, and How to Get It Right**

**Intro:** You've decided your Excel system needs to go. Maybe it broke, maybe someone quit, maybe you're just tired of the bottleneck. This guide explains what actually happens when you hire a developer to replace a spreadsheet — from the developer's perspective. What to look for, what to provide, what it costs, and what questions to ask.

**H2: First — Is This the Right Move? (Quick Reality Check)**
3 scenarios where custom development is the right call vs. where no-code tools work better:
- Custom dev is right: unique process, finance/compliance logic, multi-user with roles, needs to integrate with existing systems
- No-code is fine: standard CRM, simple database, you want to DIY
- Clear guide: "If your process has logic that no off-the-shelf tool handles, you need custom."

**H2: What to Look for in a Developer**
Honest criteria:
1. **Domain knowledge first.** A developer who has built business process software before — ideally in your industry. A generic web developer can build what you describe; a domain-aware developer builds what you actually need.
2. **Has examples of similar projects.** Not just a portfolio of websites — actual business apps, data tools, management systems.
3. **Asks the right questions.** A red flag: developer immediately talks about tech stack. A green flag: developer asks "what decisions do you need to make with this data?"
4. **Transparent about timeline and cost.** (See next section.)

**H2: What to Provide (How to Brief a Developer on Your Excel)**
Practical checklist — what actually helps:
- Walk through your Excel file column by column. Explain what each field means.
- Identify the 3 most important reports or views you need.
- List every person who will use the system and what they need to see/do.
- Identify any calculations or formulas that are business-critical.
- Note any external systems that need to connect (accounting software, payment systems, etc.)
- What does "success" look like? How will you know the new system is working?

**H2: What It Costs (Real Ranges)**
Honest breakdown — not agency-speak:
- Simple single-user tool (3-5 views, basic CRUD): €2,500–€5,000
- Multi-user app with roles, reporting, custom logic: €4,000–€8,000+
- Complex system with integrations, financial logic, audit trail: €8,000–€20,000+
- Context: a large agency quotes $45,000–$200,000 for similar work. A specialist freelancer with the right background delivers the same outcome at a fraction of the cost.

**H2: What the Process Actually Looks Like**
Step by step — demystify it:
1. Discovery call — understand the process, not the tech
2. Scope document — written summary of what gets built
3. Design/wireframes — you see it before it's built
4. Development in phases — first the core, then the reports, then extra features
5. Handover — you can use it; training if needed
6. Ongoing: bugs, additions, hosting support (retainer optional)

Timeline: 4–10 weeks for most business process apps

**H2: The Questions to Ask Any Developer Before Hiring**
- "Have you built something similar before? Can I see it?"
- "How do you handle changes during development?"
- "What happens after we launch if something breaks?"
- "Do you understand [finance/operations/the specific domain]?"
- "Can you explain what you'd build before we sign anything?"
